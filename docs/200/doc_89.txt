There were 290,660 non-citizens living in Latvia or 14.1% of Latvian residents , mainly ethnic Russians who arrived after the occupation of 1940 and their descendants . In some cities , e.g. Daugavpils and Rzekne , ethnic Latvians constitute a minority of the total population . Despite the fact that the proportion of ethnic Latvians has been steadily increasing for more than a decade , ethnic Latvians also make up slightly less than a half of the population of the capital city of Latvia Rga . The share of ethnic Latvians had fallen from 77% ( 1,467,035 ) in 1935 to 52% ( 1,387,757 ) in 1989 .
