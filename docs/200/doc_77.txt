Latvia has a long tradition of conservation . The first laws and regulations were promulgated in the 16th and 17th centuries . There are 706 specially state-level protected natural areas in Latvia , of which : 4 national parks , 1 biosphere reserve , 42 nature parks , 9 areas of protected landscapes , 260 nature reserves , 4 strict nature reserves , 355 nature monuments , 7 protected marine areas and 24 microreserves . Nationally protected areas account for 12790 km2 or around 20% of Latvia 's total land area . Latvia 's Red Book ( Endangered Species List of Latvia ) , which was established in 1977 , contains 112 plant species and 119 animal species . Latvia has ratified the international Washington , Bern , and Ramsare conventions .
