In the 1800s , it became known as Surrattsville . The main building served as the post office , an inn and tavern , and a polling place . This main residence was one of two properties owned by the widowed Mary Surratt ; the second property was in Washington , D.C. Because she was found guilty of complicity in the Lincoln assassination , Mary Surratt was hanged at the Capitol Prison in Washington D.C. , on 7 July 1865 . ( Her house is now the Surratt House Museum , with a focus on the assassination . ) The U.S. Post Office renamed the town Robeysville , due to the notoriety of the Surratt name , and in keeping with naming towns after the post master . In 1879 , Robeysville was renamed Clinton . The local high school , however , retains the name of Surrattsville , and some locals continue , in common usage , to call the town Surattsville .
