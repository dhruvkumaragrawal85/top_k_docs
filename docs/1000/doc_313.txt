For several decades beginning in the early 19th century , New London was the second busiest whaling port after New Bedford , Massachusetts in the world . The wealth that whaling brought into the city furnished the capital to fund much of the city 's present architecture . The New Haven and New London Railroad connected New London by rail to New Haven and points beyond by the 1850s .
