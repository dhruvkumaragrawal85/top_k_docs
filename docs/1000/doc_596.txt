1920s Hardin and Louis grew apart . Armstrong formed a new Hot Five , with Earl Hines on piano . Hardin reformed her own band with Freddie Keppard on cornet ( whom Hardin considered second only to Louis ) . Louis and Hardin separated in 1931 , when he had begun a liaison with Alpha Smith , who threatened to sue Armstrong for breach of promise , so he begged Hardin not to grant him a divorce . I felt sorry for Louis , Hardin later recalled , but he had two-timed me , so I gave him a divorce just to teach him a lessonand I sued him , too . # Later years # In the 1930s , sometimes billing herself as Mrs. Louis Armstrong , Hardin led an All Girl Orchestra , then a mixed gender Big Band which broadcast nationally over the NBC radio network .
