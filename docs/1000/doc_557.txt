The pair hired Jay Bentley on bass and Jay Ziskrout on drums and began writing songs . In 1981 , the band released their eponymous debut EP on the newly formed label , Epitaph Records , which was and continues to be managed and owned by Gurewitz . In 1982 , the band released their first full-length album , ' ' How Could Hell Be Any Worse ? ' ' , gaining the band a sizable following . During the recording of that album , Ziskrout left the band and was replaced by Pete Finestone . After experiencing more lineup changes and releasing their second album , ' ' Into the Unknown ' ' ( 1983 ) , to lukewarm response , Bad Religion called it quits in 1984 .
