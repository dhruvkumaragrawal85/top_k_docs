I later learned that they had taught me all the wrong things , Hardin recalled in 1971 , but they meant well . It was at Fisk University , a college for African Americans located in downtown Nashville , that Hardin was taught a more acceptable approach to the instrument . Hardin stayed at the school for one year , returning to Memphis in 1917 . In August 1918 , she moved to Chicago with her mother and stepfather . By then , she had become proficient in reading music , a skill that landed her a job as a sheet music demonstrator at Jones Music Store .
