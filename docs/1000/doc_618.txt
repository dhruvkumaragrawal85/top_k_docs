Writers such as Homer had their heroes bathe in warm water so as to regain their strength ; it is perhaps notable that the mother of Achilles bathed him in order to gain his invincibility . Palaces have been uncovered throughout Greece with areas that are dedicated to bathing , spaces with ceramic bathtubs , as well as sophisticated drainage systems . Homer uses the word , ' ' loetr ' ' , baths , later , ' ' loutr ' ' , from the verb , ' ' loein ' ' , to bathe . The same root finds an even earlier attestation on Linear B tablets , in the name of the River Lousios ( bathing river ) , in Arcadia . Public baths are mentioned by the comedian Aristophanes as , ' ' balanea ' ' ( Sing. : , ' ' balaneon ' ' , Latinized as ' ' balneum ' ' , a balneary ) .
