While cannabis use is accepted as a contributory cause of schizophrenia by some , it remains controversial , with pre-existing vulnerability to psychosis emerging as the key factor that influences the link between cannabis use and psychosis . Some studies indicate that the effects of two active compounds in cannabis , tetrahydrocannabinol ( THC ) and cannabidiol ( CBD ) , have opposite effects with respect to psychosis . While THC can induce psychotic symptoms in healthy individuals , CBD may reduce the symptoms caused by cannabis . Cannabis use has increased dramatically over the past few decades whereas the rate of psychosis has not increased . Together , these findings suggest that cannabis use may hasten the onset of psychosis in those who may already be predisposed to psychosis .
