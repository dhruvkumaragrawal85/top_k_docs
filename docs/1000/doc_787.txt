He later tried animal husbandry , then became a schoolteacher specialising in carpentry at Manyame School in Kezi . In 1942 , at the age of 25 , during his career as a teacher , he decided that he should go to South Africa to further his education , do carpentry and qualify to a higher level . He attended Adams College and the Jan H. Hofmeyr School of Social Work in South Africa . There he met Nelson Mandela and other regional nationalist leaders at the University of Fort Hare , though he did not attend that university . It was at the Jan Hofmeyr School of Social Work that he was awarded a B.A. Degree in Social Science in 1952 . Nkomo married his wife Johanna MaFuyana on 1 October 1949 .
