Deedes was made a life peer in 1986 , becoming Baron Deedes , of Aldington in the County of Kent , though he always preferred to be addressed as Bill rather than Lord Deedes . Bill Deedes continued to comment on social and political issues through his newspaper columns up to his death . In his later years , he gained a cult fanbase after two memorable appearances on ' ' Have I Got News For You ' ' becoming , at the age of 88 , the oldest guest ever to have appeared on the programme until 2012 when Baroness Trumpington appeared at the age of 90 . He was also a stalwart member of the Carlton Club and was appointed as an ambassador for UNICEF in 1998 , running high-profile campaigns against landmines .
